Notes on normalization: policies use differing terminology, so threats were mapped to consistent categories. "CBRN" is split into Chemical, Biological, and Radiological/nuclear — a full-CBRN policy earns all three, while "chemical & biological" or "biochemical" earns only the first two, and "biological weapons" only the second. "Automated AI R&D" covers AI self-improvement / ML R&D. "Loss of control / deceptive alignment" covers misalignment. "Other misuse / harmful outputs" covers malware, CSAM, and discriminatory outputs. A few cells reflect judgment calls (e.g., Microsoft's "advanced autonomy" mapped to both automated AI R&D and autonomous replication; Cohere's malware generation mapped to cyber offense).
